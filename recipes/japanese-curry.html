Japanese curry is a delicious and comforting dish that combines a rich, flavorful curry sauce with meat and vegetables. Here's a recipe for making Japanese curry:

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) meat of your choice (chicken, beef, pork), cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 2 carrots, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cups (720ml) water or chicken broth
  • 3 tablespoons Japanese curry roux (available in Asian grocery stores or online)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooked rice for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot or deep skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced onion and minced garlic, and saut√© until the onion is softened and translucent.
  2. Add the meat to the pot and cook until it is browned on all sides.
  3. Once the meat is browned, add the potatoes, carrots, and any additional vegetables you're using to the pot.
  4. liPour in the water or chicken broth, making sure the meat and vegetables are covered. Bring the liquid to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Once the vegetables are tender, add the Japanese curry roux to the pot. Stir well to dissolve the roux into the liquid.
  6. Add the soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, ketchup, and sugar to the pot. Stir to combine.
  7. Let the curry simmer for another 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened and the flavors have melded together.
  8. Season the curry with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Serve the Japanese curry hot over cooked rice.
