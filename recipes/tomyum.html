Tomyum

Tom Yum, also known as Tom Yam, is a popular Thai soup renowned for its bold and complex flavors.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 3-4 slices of galangal (or ginger if unavailable)
  • 2-3 Thai bird's eye chilies, sliced (adjust according to spice preference)
  • 200g (7 oz) shrimp, chicken, or tofu, sliced or cubed
  • 200g (7 oz) mushrooms, sliced (such as straw mushrooms or button mushrooms)
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Handful of fresh cilantro leaves, chopped
  • Optional: Thai chili paste (Nam Prik Pao) for extra flavor

Instructions

  1. In a pot, bring the chicken or vegetable broth to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. Add lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal (or ginger), and Thai bird's eye chilies to the pot. Let it simmer for about 5-10 minutes to infuse the broth with flavors.
  3. Add shrimp, chicken, or tofu to the pot and let it cook until the protein is almost cooked through.
  4. Add mushrooms, tomatoes, and onion to the pot. Let it simmer for another 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Season the soup with fish sauce, lime juice, and sugar. Adjust the seasoning according to your taste preference. If you want to add more depth of flavor, you can also stir in a spoonful of Thai chili paste (Nam Prik Pao) at this stage.
  6. Once everything is cooked and seasoned to your liking, remove the pot from heat.
  7. Serve the Tom Yum soup hot, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves.
